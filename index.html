Help Fight Traffic in Cupertino

Let's Stop Excessive Traffic In Our city

Are you ever excited to head home after a long day at De Anza, but still find yourself stuck in Cupertino an hour later?

The problem with traffic is more than just about time. It has caused many accidents and tragedies in cupertino. Young children have lost their lives because of poor traffic control. People constantly block intersections and at times it’s not their fault. Something has to be done in order to make living in Cupertino safer all the while making it easier to travel through the city.

Police activity around the area was increased to make sure there are no incidents where trucks or other vehicles are breaking the law.

The roads on McClellan have been made narrower by installing a bike lane barrier, although this reduces the risk of an accident with a bicyclist it makes driving in the lane very difficult which can increase in the likelihood of a collision with other vehicles. So the roads should be wider overall to ensure safety of both vehicles and bicyclists.

Future actions are being planned to increase options for public transport. The bart service is going to be extended from Alameda County to Santa Clara county with stops at Milpitas Berryessa/North San Jose, service is planned to begin in 2020.

Over the years in Cupertino heavy traffic has taken many innocent lives. In 2014 a Monta Vista High School student was hit by a big rig and killed. This sparked the movement for change to improve traffic safety in Cupertino.

Proposed Solutions

Commuter Train

In Cupertino there is a freight train that passes once a week. Most of the time the train tracks are never used. We plan to use the already built infrastructure to add public transport. We are proposing for a VTA train or a CALtrain to be added on the line adding another method of public transportation for cupertino residents. This can also help reduce the amount of traffic around school times as three major schools are extremely close to the tracks.

Cupertino Local Rideshare App

An app can be created to use one’s GPS to find others going to the same destination/area as you. There will be many categories for commute such as work, school, food, gym, etc. This will reduce the amount of cars on the road since people will carpool and make people’s commutes more pleasant for them.

Companies can give incentive to use more rideshare apps by offering to pay for it every month. The people who are going to be driving for the app should also be reimbursed for gas on any of shared trips but not singular rides.

Conclusion

Think about a Bay Area where you feel very safe to drive and aren’t putting anyone at risk. Amazing thought right? The only thing we need to accomplish this is mindfulness and support of the people. Avoid the road when you can, and spread the message!

Traffic is not just one person’s problem, it’s everyone’s. We all have a duty in society to help each other to benefit us all. The fight against a city with traffic jams will not be easy to overcome but we all must come together to reduce the amount of traffic in our cities. Imagine a Bay Area where you feel safe to drive and for your kids to bike. We can all accomplish this but it starts with YOU!
